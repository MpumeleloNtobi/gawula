# Moderator Guide

## Moderator Principles

1. Do not pitch Gawula until the concept-card section.
2. Do not ask whether participants want multi-store ordering until after they describe real ordering pain.
3. Ask for specific stories, not opinions in the abstract.
4. Follow emotion: anxiety, compromise, unfairness, delight, confusion, and trust are the signal.
5. Ask what they did next, not only what they wanted.

## Opening Script

Thanks for joining. Today we are talking about food delivery apps like Uber Eats and Mr D. We want to understand what works, what feels frustrating, and what you wish these apps let you do. We are not here to sell anything, and there are no right answers. Please speak from real experiences, especially recent orders.

We will record the session only for research notes. Your feedback will be summarized anonymously.

## Warm-Up: Last Order

Ask each participant:

1. Which app did you last use: Uber Eats, Mr D, or another app?
2. What did you order?
3. Who was the order for?
4. Why did you choose that app and restaurant?
5. What worked well?

Follow-ups:

1. What made the decision easy?
2. What nearly stopped you from ordering?
3. Did anyone else influence the choice?

## Journey Mapping

For 2-3 participants, map the full order:

1. Craving or need.
2. Opening the app.
3. Search/browse.
4. Restaurant choice.
5. Cart and checkout.
6. Payment.
7. Waiting/tracking.
8. Delivery handoff.
9. Eating/opening the bag.
10. Support/refund if relevant.

Ask:

1. Where did you feel most confident?
2. Where did you feel most uncertain?
3. Where did the app surprise you?
4. Where did you spend more than expected?
5. What would you change about that moment?

## Compromise And Add-On Moments

Ask:

1. Tell me about a time you ordered for more than one person.
2. How did you decide which restaurant won?
3. Who compromised?
4. Did anyone order separately?
5. Have you ever wanted something from a different place after choosing the main restaurant?
6. Have you ever paid another delivery fee for drinks, dessert, snacks, groceries, or a forgotten item?
7. Have you ever decided not to order something because it meant starting a separate order?

Probe for:

1. Families with different food preferences.
2. Office lunch orders.
3. Late-night group orders.
4. Food-court or mall behavior.
5. Add-ons after the first item is added.
6. Delivery-fee frustration.

## Feature Wish-Listing

Ask after each pain point:

1. What do you wish the app let you do at that moment?
2. What would have made that easier?
3. Would you expect the restaurant, driver, or app to solve it?
4. Would you pay extra for that? Why or why not?
5. Would that make you switch apps for one order?

Do not correct participants. Capture their language exactly.

## Tradeoff Ranking

Give each participant 5 votes. Ask them to spend votes on the features they value most.

Feature options:

1. Cheaper delivery.
2. More accurate delivery time.
3. Easier refunds.
4. Stronger delivery proof.
5. Better driver contact.
6. Add forgotten items before the order locks.
7. One basket for multiple stores in the same food court or hub.
8. Group order where everyone can choose from different stores.
9. Better promos and bundles.
10. Clearer fee breakdown.
11. Better restaurant accountability for missing/wrong items.
12. Reorder previous group or mixed meals.

Ask:

1. Why did you vote for those?
2. Which feature sounds nice but would not change your behavior?
3. Which one would make you try a new app?
4. Which one would worry you?

## Concept Card Reaction

Show each concept card one at a time. Ask:

1. What do you think this means?
2. When would you use it?
3. When would you not use it?
4. What would you need to trust it?
5. What could go wrong?
6. Would this make you choose this app over Uber Eats or Mr D?
7. What would you call this feature?

## Closing

Ask:

1. If you could change one thing about food delivery apps tomorrow, what would it be?
2. What would make you recommend a new food delivery app to someone?
3. What should a new app never get wrong?

Thank participants and explain that their feedback will be anonymized and used to improve the product direction.
